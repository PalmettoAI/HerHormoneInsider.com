Online HRT providers, scored side-by-side

Six independent reviewers ran the intake at every major menopause-focused telehealth platform between January and April 2026. We scored on prescribing range, clinician credentials, price transparency, and the operational fine print.

Our methodology, in brief: each reviewer presented the same clinical scenario — 47-year-old, classic vasomotor and sleep symptoms, no contraindications — and tracked what regimen was offered, what labs were requested, and what the all-in 12-month cost would be. How we scored

The composite score is a weighted average of: prescribing breadth (30%), clinician credentials (25%), price transparency (20%), insurance handling (15%), and exit terms (10%). A perfect 10 would require: full FDA-approved prescribing range including transdermal estradiol, micronized progesterone, vaginal estrogen, and testosterone; NCMP-certified physicians as the primary visit type; published flat pricing for visits and medications; in-network with at least three of the four largest commercial payers; and easy chart download plus prescription transfer on cancellation.

Important caveats

  • None of these platforms substitute for the in-person workup recommended for women with complex risk profiles. See our perimenopause guide for who should not start with telehealth.
  • Prices reflect April 2026 published rates; promotional pricing has been excluded.
  • Where a platform offers both physician and nurse-practitioner visit types, the scoring reflects the default visit type at intake.
